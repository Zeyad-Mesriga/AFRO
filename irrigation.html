Agriculture

Cultivation stages:

Agricultural management is an important matter,
as the farmer takes several things into consideration.
He needs to understand the basic stages of plant production, the most important of which are:

  1. Crop selection:

    Types of agricultural crops vary,
    and there are several factors that help in choosing the appropriate crop for agriculture, including:

    • Its high production capacity: The farmer is interested in choosing the productive variety, primarily for profit goals.
    • Selection of the variety according to the region: The variety that grows within the conditions of the region is selected, with the help of the expertise of the Ministry of Agriculture.
    • Crop quality: The farmer is interested in choosing high-quality varieties, which increases production efficiency and profit.
    • Resistance to ambient conditions: The resistance of crops depends on their genetic composition, such that they resist difficult conditions and diseases.
    • Choosing early-maturing varieties: Early or medium-maturing varieties are chosen because they are less susceptible to diseases and weather changes than late-maturing crops.

  2. Preparing the land for planting:

    The process of preparing the ground before planting it is considered essential, to provide a suitable medium for the plant roots, as it facilitates their passage in the soil, and provides properties that help the seeds absorb water and nutrients from the soil, and grow, and any previously planted plants are removed, so that they do not deplete the nutrients, and the preparation processes differ. The land depends on the region and the crops, as it gives the plants better results when prepared in the right ways and at appropriate times. The stages of preparing the land for agriculture include:

    • Plowing: It is the process of preparing the land for seed incubation by loosening and stimulating it. There are several factors that affect the efficiency of plowing, including:
      • Type of land: Cohesive agricultural lands require breaking up and smoothing operations, and they also need powerful agricultural machines, unlike light lands.
      • Type of previous crop: Residues from previous crops vary from one crop to another, in terms of root depth and soil cohesion. Some residues from previous crops need to increase the number of plowing times, and some residues from previous crops may be used as fertilizer for the new crop, so the land needs early plowing.
      • Type of crop to be planted: Crops differ from each other in terms of the depth of plowing and their preparation for seed nursery.
      • Types of weeds and their extent: The soil needs early plowing when there are many weeds in the ground, so that they do not consume the nutrients of the crop to be grown.
      • Moisture percentage in the soil: High soil moisture leads to the formation of cohesive blocks of soil, which requires increased plowing efficiency, while dry soil requires less efficient plowing.
    • Land leveling: The process of breaking up the soil well, so that water is better distributed through irrigation pipes.
    • Solar sterilization of the land: Raising the temperature of the soil covered with polyethylene leads to the elimination of insects, pathogens, and harmful weeds in the soil.
    • Land planning and laying irrigation pipes: Depending on the type of crops, the land is planned, leaving a distance between each crop and the other that is appropriate for its type.
    • Determining planting sites: A single line of planting sites is determined by irrigating the land for a period, then digging in places where water collects to plant seeds.

  3. Seed selection:

    Highly efficient seeds are selected for planting for several reasons, including:

    • Producing healthier seedlings.
    • Increasing the speed of root growth, which leads to faster utilization of nutrients from the soil.
    • Increasing agricultural crop productivity.

  4. Irrigation:

    Irrigation is very important for the purposes of agricultural production, as the irrigation process affects the entire agricultural process, starting from the stage of seed preparation, then germination, root growth, use of nutrients, and quality of products. Among the most important things that are taken into consideration are:

    • The value of irrigation: Irrigation has many benefits, including:
      • Grow more herbs and crops.
      • Producing crops and herbs of greater quality, as irrigation greatly affects the quality of agricultural products.
      • Irrigation extends agricultural seasons and helps start agricultural seasons earlier.
      • Marketing plants outside their agricultural seasons.
      • Irrigation is important to enhance the benefits of using fertilizers and facilitate plant growth.
    • Choosing an irrigation system: The irrigation methods and methods used vary, and the choice of a particular type over another is due to certain reasons, including:
      • Different types of soil used in agriculture.
      • Topography of cultivated land.
      • Water abundance and its sources.
      • Area of ​​cultivated land.
      • The amount of water that can be stored on the farm.
      • The number of manpower and the budget allocated to agriculture.
      • Availability of energy sources.
    • Irrigation scheduling: Irrigation scheduling determines the appropriate time for irrigation and the amount of water that the soil needs, by determining the crops’ water needs at the various stages of their growth and in the various climatic conditions they face. To know the amount of available water and the amount of it consumed by plants, it is recommended to follow the following:
      • Monitor plants constantly.
      • Monitoring the shape and texture of the soil
      • Use appropriate measuring devices to determine the humidity level.

  5. Fertilization:

    Fertilizer is an organic or inorganic compound that contains the nutrients necessary for the growth of agricultural crops. It is placed on the soil, combined with it, or by placing it directly on the plant. The farmer needs to test the soil to determine the nutrients available to the plant, in order to provide fertilizer. Necessary to achieve optimal crop production, so that farmers’ profit potential depends on it. There are at least 17 essential elements for plant growth, their source is either water and air or soil and fertilizers, as well as nutrients and micronutrients.

  6. harvest:

    Harvesting is the process of removing agricultural crops after they have matured for processing, consumption, or storage. Some root crops may be left in the soil and harvested when needed, but most crops after their maturity are at risk of deterioration if left exposed to nutrients.

  7. Agriculture tools:

    There are many tools used in agriculture, and choosing the most appropriate ones provides better returns and results, and may contribute to improving production and increasing profits, the most important of which are:

    • Shovel: A tool used to remove weeds from the ground.
    • Iron roller: Iron roller:
    • Axe: A tool used to cut down trees.
    • Animal plow: A tool drawn by animals for plowing the land, turning the soil, and pulling weeds.
    • Earth rake: A tool used to remove stones and impurities from the ground. It is an iron piece mounted on a wooden stick and has teeth.
    • Pitchfork: A tool used to separate crop grains from hay in the air. It is a stick with wooden fingers.
    • Sieve: A tool used to purify small grains from impurities. It is cylindrical, similar to a sieve, and consists of a base entwined with taut threads that prevent the passage of impurities.
    • Sickle: A tool used to remove branches of weeds.
    • Wheelbarrow: A tool used to transport small loads over short distances. It usually consists of only one wheel, and has multiple uses, including transporting crops.

Irrigation methods

Irrigation with sprinklers

  • The use of sprinkler irrigation began in the late 20th century, and its spread increased after World War II after the efficiency of pumps, pipes, and light-duty sprinklers increased. In this method, water is sprayed into the air through small holes in the pipes until it falls on the surface of the soil and plants in the form of rain mist.
  • The advantage of this method of irrigation with sprinklers is that it enables farmers to add fertilizer and pesticides to the water. It can also be used on uneven lands. It does not require labor, protects plants from frost, and works to maintain their temperature.
  • One of the harms of this method is that it leads to the appearance of salts on the surface of the soil.

Drip irrigation

  • This method depends on irrigating the plants with water in the form of drops directly under the plants, where a network of pipes designated for drip irrigation is designed and distributed among the trees. At the bottom of each tree there is a hole in the pipe from which water emerges in the form of drops.
  • The drip irrigation method is suitable for sandy and desert land, and it is very water-saving, giving the trees their water needs without wasting.
  • However, one of the disadvantages of this method is that constructing irrigation networks is expensive, and preparing the network requires highly qualified workers.

Surface irrigation

  • It is considered one of the most common methods, and it is not a modern method, but it is effective. This method depends on designing a network of regular grooves and channels around the trees, and making the water flow through these channels to run through them and reach all the trees, with the necessity of having a drainage system at the end of the waterway.
  • The surface irrigation method has several advantages: As it is easy to use, its cost is low, and it does not require trained workers.
  • One of the harms of this method is that it leads to the appearance of salts on the surface of the soil.

Bubble irrigation

  • Bubble irrigation can be defined as an irrigation system that combines drip irrigation and irrigation with sprinklers. This method relies on injecting air into water pipes or tanks, and thus it increases the percentage of oxygen in the water by approximately 150% and thus increases its percentage in the soil, which helps the soil get rid of bacteria. harmful and improve the soil.
  • This system uses a bubble device that regulates the air flow to the water pipes before starting watering.

Subterranean irrigation methods

  • In this irrigation method, the necessary humidity for the plant’s root environment is achieved by controlling the constant level. For this purpose, it is necessary to have a sealing layer at an appropriate depth from the soil surface to control the constant surface. Therefore, this method usually makes the soil surface not wet as long as water is provided in the root environment, where the water level is raised to a high degree so that moisture can reach the root environment using the capillary action.
  • Irrigation with groundwater allows the distribution of unsaturated moisture in the root zone of the plant. One of the new systems of this method is the use of sponge-like rubber tubes under very low pressure to distribute water evenly under control and by installing it in the root zone of the plant. It creates moisture within the field soil capacity. Choosing the appropriate depth and the optimal distance for these tubes from each other and to the plant depends on the extent of moisture generated by the tube in the soil.
  • Permeable pipes are not perforated but spongy. In other words, in a permeable system there is no water flow but a moisture space is created around the pipe. The permeable technology is that with very low pressure the water seeps out from the wall of the permeable pipe and after creating a relative nice and uniform humid atmosphere up to To a state of equilibrium with its surroundings the water will be absorbed by the root and the pipe will leak in and we will not have any evaporation in the system.
